Plain-English interpretation

Significant. Income is a positive, statistically significant predictor of voter turnout (p < 0.001).

For every $10,000 increase in income, predicted turnout rises by about 3.4 percentage points, holding age constant.

The model explains roughly 41% of the variation in turnout — moderate for survey data. Consider adding education as a control.
